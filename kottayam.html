Kottayam


Kottayam is located in South-West Kerala in the basin of the Meenachil River at an average elevation of 3 metres (9.8 ft) above sea level, and has a moderate climate. It is known for its trade in natural rubber, and the national Rubber Board is headquartered in the city, as is the Plantation Corporation of Kerala. Kottayam Port is India's first multi-modal inland container depot. The lakeside resort village of Kumarakom is located just 15 km (9.3 mi) from town, and draws thousands of domestic and international tourists annually. Houseboats and fresh water fish are the major attractions.

Here are some places to visit:

  • Illikkal Kallu
  • Elaveezhapoonchira
  • Marmala Waterfall
  • Vaikom Mahadeva Temple
  • Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple
